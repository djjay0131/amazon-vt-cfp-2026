# Project Brief: Amazon-VT CFP 2026-2027

## Core Objective

Develop and submit a competitive research proposal to the **Amazon-Virginia Tech Initiative 2026-2027 Call for Proposals**, securing funding for an AI research project and/or doctoral fellowship.

## CFP Context

The Amazon-VT Initiative bridges academic and industry research in artificial intelligence. It annually funds multiple research projects and doctoral fellowships. Proposals must advance research in one or more designated topic areas.

## Topics of Interest

Proposers select primary and secondary categories:

1. **Foundation Model Improvements** — Novel architectures, training algorithms, multi-modal, multi-lingual, continual learning, RL methodologies
2. **Foundation Model/Agentic Evaluation** — New benchmarks, robust evaluation of GenAI systems and agents
3. **Efficient Generative AI & Inference** — Cloud/on-device efficiency, long context, inference-time scaling, speculative sampling
4. **Reasoning** — Commonsense, domain-specific (math, coding), temporal/spatial, planning
5. **Knowledge Grounding** — Multi-modal grounding, memory-augmented AI, multimodal retrieval, knowledge fusion, LLM-knowledge interaction
6. **Agentic AI** — Autonomous systems, multi-agent orchestration, agent customization, agent-environment simulation, agentic applications
7. **Personalization** — System-level, dialog, prompt-based, personalized RAG
8. **Responsible Generative AI** — Red teaming, robustness, guardrails, frontier risks, cultural alignment
9. **AI Accelerated Science & Engineering** — GenAI for physics, math, chemistry, biology, hardware, materials, engineering, economics, healthcare, climate

## Deliverables

### Phase 1: Abstract (Due March 13, 2026)
- 1-page abstract (excluding references)
- Research project idea overview
- Primary and secondary topic categories
- Submitted via Amazon research portal

### Phase 2: Full Proposal (Due April 27, 2026 — if invited)
- 3-4 pages plus references
- Detailed research plan
- Format details shared April 6, 2026

## Success Criteria

- [ ] Compelling research problem with clear novelty
- [ ] Strong alignment with CFP topic areas (primary + secondary)
- [ ] Feasible methodology and timeline (1-year project)
- [ ] Clear potential for Amazon-relevant impact
- [ ] Abstract selected for full proposal invitation
- [ ] Full proposal funded
