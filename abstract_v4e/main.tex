\noindent\textbf{Primary Topic:} AI Accelerated Science \& Engineering \hfill
\textbf{Secondary:} Agentic AI

\vspace{0.5em}

AI-driven scientific breakthroughs increasingly rely on tightly coupling learned models with structured knowledge and simulation~\cite{Abramson2024AlphaFold3,Merchant2023GNoME}. However, large language models (LLMs) remain fundamentally misaligned with engineering practice: they hallucinate physical quantities~\cite{Ji2023HallucinationSurvey}, fail to enforce constraints, and lack grounding in the structured ontologies and simulation feedback loops that govern real-world systems. While recent advances in knowledge-grounded generation---combining retrieval~\cite{Lewis2020RAG}, knowledge graphs~\cite{Edge2024GraphRAG}, and multi-source data integration---improve factual consistency, they do not address a critical gap: the absence of rigorous validation for AI-generated engineering outputs.

This project proposes \textbf{Agentic Knowledge Graph Engineering (AKG-E)}, a domain-agnostic framework integrating knowledge-grounded reasoning with simulation-in-the-loop validation and Verification, Validation, and Uncertainty Quantification (VVUQ)~\cite{Oberkampf2010VVUQ}. AKG-E unifies three layers: (1)~a \textit{domain ontology layer} encoding engineering knowledge graphs with entities, constraints, and physical laws; (2)~a \textit{simulation integration layer} exposing domain tools (e.g., finite element analysis~\cite{Logg2012FEniCS}, digital twin microsimulation~\cite{Tao2019DigitalTwin}) as agent-callable interfaces; and (3)~a \textit{VVUQ validation layer} that verifies outputs against governing equations, quantifies uncertainty via propagation through agent pipelines, and enforces regulatory and physical constraints. Agents orchestrate across retrieval-augmented generation, structured graph queries, and simulation feedback, extending industry knowledge-grounding approaches (e.g., Amazon-scale knowledge graph systems~\cite{Yu2024COSMO}) with physics-based validation.

In construction engineering~\cite{Eastman2011BIM}, for example, an agent generates a structural design from a BIM model using knowledge-grounded retrieval; AKG-E then invokes finite element simulation to verify load constraints, propagates uncertainty through material assumptions, and either certifies the design or iteratively refines it under constraint violations. This simulation-grounded loop transforms generative outputs into validated, decision-ready artifacts.

We evaluate AKG-E across construction (primary), transportation, and energy domains, measuring (i)~constraint violation reduction relative to LLM-only and RAG baselines, (ii)~calibration of uncertainty estimates, and (iii)~cross-domain generalization under a shared architecture. We hypothesize $>$50\% reduction in constraint violations and well-calibrated uncertainty bounds (within 10\% of nominal coverage). Expected outcomes include a reusable AKG-E framework, domain-adaptable knowledge graph templates, and VVUQ benchmarks for agentic systems.

By elevating validation to a first-class component of AI reasoning, AKG-E advances key knowledge grounding challenges---including multi-source retrieval, knowledge fusion, and interaction between LLMs and external systems---while introducing a generalizable methodology for trustworthy, simulation-grounded AI in engineering.
