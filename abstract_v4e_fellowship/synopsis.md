# Fellowship Submission Synopsis

**For portal short-summary field. Applicant: Jason Cusati. Advisor: Chris Brown.**
**Topic:** AI Accelerated Science & Engineering Innovation (primary) · Agentic AI (secondary)

## 3-sentence synopsis (~75 words)

Agentic Knowledge Graphs for AI-Accelerated Engineering (AKG-E) couples agentic AI with structured engineering knowledge graphs and physics-based simulation under a Verification, Validation, and Uncertainty Quantification (VVUQ) gate, turning generative outputs into certified, decision-ready engineering artifacts. The proposed research synthesizes four concrete prior threads at Virginia Tech—an agentic research-progression KG system, a Construction-AI material-takeoff toolset, a real-time transportation safety index over multimodal sensors, and graduate VVUQ coursework grounded in the Oberkampf–Roy formulation—into a domain-agnostic framework that targets deployment via Amazon Bedrock Knowledge Bases. The fellowship year would deliver an open-source AKG-E framework, domain-adaptable KG ontologies, and a reproducible benchmark suite measuring constraint-violation reduction versus LLM-only baselines.

## 2-sentence variant (~50 words, if a tighter field is required)

Agentic Knowledge Graphs for AI-Accelerated Engineering (AKG-E) closes the verification gap that blocks generative AI from engineering deployment by combining domain knowledge graphs, physics-based simulation, and a VVUQ validation layer into a single agent-orchestrated framework. Synthesized from four prior projects at Virginia Tech and targeting Amazon Bedrock deployment, AKG-E delivers an open framework, KG ontologies, and reproducible benchmarks for trustworthy agentic engineering AI.
