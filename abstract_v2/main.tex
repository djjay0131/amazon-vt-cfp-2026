\noindent\textbf{Primary Topic:} Knowledge Grounding \hfill
\textbf{Secondary Topic:} Agentic AI

\vspace{0.5em}

Retrieval-augmented generation (RAG)~\cite{Lewis2020RAG} has become a foundational technique for grounding large language model (LLM) inference in external knowledge. Production systems have matured substantially: hybrid retrieval combining semantic (dense) and keyword (sparse) search is now standard practice~\cite{AWSBedrockHybridSearch2024}, and retrieval is increasingly exposed as a tool within agentic workflows where models decide \textit{when} and \textit{what} to retrieve~\cite{OpenAIFunctionCalling2025}. Yet despite these advances, two critical gaps remain. First, even state-of-the-art retrieval systems lack structured reasoning substrates capable of multi-hop synthesis across heterogeneous knowledge sources---a gap that graph-based approaches like GraphRAG~\cite{Edge2024GraphRAG} begin to address but do not fully resolve. Second, current systems provide limited provenance: they can cite documents, but cannot produce fine-grained, verifiable evidence chains linking individual model outputs to specific supporting knowledge, and they lack validation mechanisms that detect and recover from retrieval failures~\cite{Yan2024CRAG,Ji2023HallucinationSurvey}.

This project proposes \textbf{Provenance-Aware Agentic Knowledge Graph Systems (PA-AKG)}, an architecture that unifies structured knowledge graph reasoning, adaptive multi-source retrieval, and agent-driven validation into a single grounding substrate---capabilities that existing systems provide only in isolation~\cite{Edge2024GraphRAG,Yan2024CRAG,Asai2024SelfRAG}. The core contribution is a framework in which agents reason over entities, relationships, and structured constraints while maintaining explicit, verifiable evidence chains from outputs to specific supporting knowledge. Agents dynamically select among retrieval pathways---dense, sparse, structured graph queries, and hybrid combinations---based on task context and confidence, enabling reasoning workflows that balance semantic recall with symbolic precision.

The proposed system enables \textbf{multi-source knowledge grounding} across knowledge graphs, relational databases, scientific literature, and domain-specific document collections. A critical component is an automated \textbf{provenance generation and validation layer} that produces fine-grained evidence links from model outputs to specific supporting knowledge sources---going beyond document-level citation toward span- and entity-level traceability. Provenance signals are structured to align with emerging observability standards~\cite{OpenTelemetryGenAI2024}, making agent reasoning auditable across tool calls and retrieval actions. Validation agents monitor retrieval quality and trigger corrective actions when evidence is weak, contradictory, or insufficient, extending retrieval-control mechanisms~\cite{Yan2024CRAG,Asai2024SelfRAG} from unstructured document retrieval to structured knowledge graph reasoning.

This research investigates: (1)~how heterogeneous knowledge sources can be unified into a grounding framework supporting both semantic and structured retrieval; (2)~how agent workflows should route and validate across retrieval pathways under uncertainty; (3)~what mechanisms enable scalable fine-grained provenance generation tied to specific knowledge entities; and (4)~whether provenance-aware KG grounding yields measurable gains in reasoning accuracy, output verifiability, and retrieval failure recovery relative to hybrid RAG baselines.

We will implement the PA-AKG architecture with integrated validation and provenance enforcement, and develop benchmarks measuring grounded reasoning quality, evidence completeness, and retrieval failure recovery. Experiments compare vector-based RAG, hybrid retrieval, GraphRAG-style reasoning, and PA-AKG across \textbf{multi-hop QA over heterogeneous knowledge bases, claim verification with evidence chain reconstruction, and engineering decision-support} tasks. Expected outcomes include the PA-AKG open prototype, provenance-aware reasoning benchmarks, and baselines advancing \textbf{reliable, transparent, and auditable agentic AI}.
