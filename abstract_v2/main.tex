\noindent\textbf{Primary Topic:} Knowledge Grounding \hfill \\
\textbf{Secondary Topic:} Agentic AI

\vspace{0.5em}

Retrieval-augmented generation (RAG)~\cite{Lewis2020RAG} has become a foundational technique for grounding large language model (LLM) inference in external knowledge. Production systems have matured substantially: hybrid retrieval combining semantic (dense) and keyword (sparse) search is now standard practice~\cite{AWSBedrockHybridSearch2024}, and retrieval is increasingly exposed as a tool within agentic workflows where models decide \textit{when} and \textit{what} to retrieve~\cite{OpenAIFunctionCalling2025}. Yet despite these advances, two critical gaps remain. First, even state-of-the-art retrieval systems lack structured reasoning substrates capable of multi-hop synthesis across heterogeneous knowledge sources---a gap that graph-based approaches like GraphRAG~\cite{Edge2024GraphRAG} begin to address but do not fully resolve. Second, current systems provide limited provenance: they can cite documents, but cannot produce fine-grained, verifiable evidence chains linking individual model outputs to specific supporting knowledge, and they lack validation mechanisms that detect and recover from retrieval failures~\cite{Yan2024CRAG,Ji2023HallucinationSurvey}.

This project proposes \textbf{Provenance-Aware Agentic Knowledge Graph Systems (PA-AKG)}. The core novelty is that PA-AKG builds a \textit{provenance graph} that links every output span to specific KG entities with confidence scores, and deploys validation agents that use this provenance to detect and recover from retrieval failures in real time---no existing system combines structured KG reasoning, adaptive multi-source retrieval, and agent-driven validation in a single grounding substrate~\cite{Edge2024GraphRAG,Yan2024CRAG,Asai2024SelfRAG}. For a multi-hop claim, the provenance graph records which entities, relationships, and source spans contributed at each link, enabling fine-grained verification well beyond document-level citations. Agents dynamically route among dense, sparse, and structured KG pathways based on task context and provenance completeness; validation agents assess evidence sufficiency using provenance signals aligned with observability standards~\cite{OpenTelemetryGenAI2024}, triggering corrective retrieval when grounding is weak or contradictory---extending retrieval-control mechanisms~\cite{Yan2024CRAG,Asai2024SelfRAG} from unstructured documents to structured KG reasoning.

We will prototype PA-AKG on Amazon Bedrock Knowledge Bases~\cite{AWSBedrockHybridSearch2024}, using hybrid search as the retrieval layer and validating against the KG provenance backbone. Experiments compare vector-based RAG, hybrid retrieval, GraphRAG-style reasoning, and PA-AKG across \textbf{multi-hop QA over heterogeneous knowledge bases} and \textbf{claim verification with evidence chain reconstruction}. Phase~1 targets multi-hop QA and claim verification; Phase~2 extends to engineering decision-support. Expected outcomes include the PA-AKG open prototype, provenance-aware benchmarks, and baselines demonstrating that \textbf{structured provenance-aware grounding} yields measurable gains in accuracy, verifiability, and failure recovery.
