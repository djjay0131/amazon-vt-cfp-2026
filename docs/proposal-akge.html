Agentic Knowledge Graphs for AI-Accelerated Engineering with Simulation-Grounded Validation

Problem Statement

AI-driven scientific breakthroughs increasingly rely on tightly coupling learned models with structured knowledge and simulation. Engineering disciplines face an analogous opportunity, yet large language models remain fundamentally misaligned with engineering practice: they hallucinate physical quantities, fail to enforce constraints, and lack grounding in the structured ontologies and simulation feedback loops that govern real-world systems.

Application Challenge

Critical gap: While knowledge-grounded generation — combining retrieval, knowledge graphs, and multi-source integration — improves factual consistency, no established methodology exists for validating AI-generated engineering outputs against physical laws and regulatory constraints. This trust gap blocks deployment in any safety-critical engineering context.

Research Questions

  1. RQ1: How can VVUQ (Verification, Validation, and Uncertainty Quantification) methodology be adapted to validate AI agent-generated engineering outputs, providing quantified uncertainty bounds and enforceable physical and regulatory constraints?
  2. RQ2: Does a domain-agnostic AKG-E architecture yield measurable constraint violation reductions across engineering domains relative to LLM-only and RAG-only baselines?

Proposed Approach — Three Layers

AKG-E applies three shared layers uniformly across engineering domains. Only the domain knowledge graph and simulator are swapped per domain; agent orchestration and VVUQ validation are reused unchanged.

  • Domain Ontology Layer — Engineering KGs encoding entities, constraints, and physical laws (pluggable per domain)
  • Simulation Integration Layer — FEA solvers, digital twin microsimulators, PDE solvers exposed as typed agent-callable interfaces
  • VVUQ Validation Layer — Verifies outputs against governing equations, quantifies uncertainty via Monte Carlo propagation, enforces regulatory constraints

Applications

Primary Pilot: Construction Engineering

Agents parse BIM/IFC models, perform automated material takeoff, invoke FEA for structural verification, and flag schedule conflicts — all gated through VVUQ until load constraints and building codes are satisfied.

Extension: Transportation Engineering

Agents build and query digital twin models of traffic networks, optimize signal timing, and validate through microsimulation — with VVUQ verifying flow capacity constraints and quantifying demand uncertainty.

Expandability

Construction is the primary pilot, but the architecture is explicitly domain-agnostic. The same three layers apply to transportation, energy, and further domains by swapping only the domain KG and simulation interface — orchestration and VVUQ layers are reused unchanged. This design directly addresses reviewer concerns about narrowness.

Intellectual Merit & Contributions

  • First principled VVUQ methodology for agentic AI outputs — a generalizable contribution separating domain-specific knowledge from shared reasoning and validation logic
  • Domain-agnostic pluggable architecture enabling cross-domain reuse
  • Reproducible VVUQ benchmarks for simulation-grounded agentic systems

Practice Impact

AKG-E transforms generative outputs into certified, decision-ready engineering artifacts — reducing hallucinated designs, enforcing code compliance, and enabling principled human-in-the-loop thresholds for autonomous versus reviewed decisions. We hypothesize >50% constraint violation reduction versus unconstrained LLM baselines.
