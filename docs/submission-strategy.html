Overlap risk with PA-AKG: Both AKG-E and PA-AKG share "Agentic AI" as a secondary topic. Submitting both to Amazon risks reviewers seeing two variations of the same agentic knowledge graph idea. SVF covers entirely different categories with zero overlap.

Three Reasons to Pick SVF over PA-AKG for Amazon

1. Topic Differentiation — No Overlap with AKG-E

SVF covers Agentic Evaluation + Responsible AI — two CFP categories completely distinct from AKG-E's Science/Engineering + Agentic AI. This means:

  • 4 of 9 CFP topics covered vs. 3 of 9 with PA-AKG
  • Different reviewer pools for each submission
  • No risk of being seen as "two flavors of the same KG idea"

2. Amazon Strategic Alignment — Evaluation is the Bottleneck

Amazon is deploying agents at scale (Bedrock AgentCore, multi-agent orchestration). Their biggest challenge isn't building more RAG systems — it's knowing whether deployed agents work reliably.

  • SVF directly addresses evaluation infrastructure for agentic systems
  • PA-AKG's core (hybrid retrieval, KG-RAG) is increasingly productized at Amazon — Bedrock Knowledge Bases already has hybrid search, citations, and reranking
  • SVF's "Responsible AI" secondary aligns with Amazon's visible investment in Bedrock Guardrails
  • Evaluation has higher novelty headroom — fewer competing proposals in this space

3. PA-AKG Has More Portability to Other Initiatives

Knowledge Grounding and RAG are broadly funded research areas with many channels — NSF, DARPA, other industry programs. PA-AKG is a strong, self-contained proposal that doesn't need Amazon-specific framing.

  • SVF's evaluation + responsible AI angle is more niche and harder to place elsewhere
  • The Amazon CFP is a better home for SVF specifically
  • PA-AKG's generalizability makes it competitive in any AI/NLP venue

Proposal Summaries

AKG-E — Agentic Knowledge Graphs for AI-Accelerated Engineering

Core idea: Domain-agnostic framework enabling LLM agents to reason over structured engineering knowledge, interact with physics-based simulations, and validate outputs against domain constraints.

Three layers: Domain ontology (KGs with constraints/physical laws), Simulation integration (PDE solvers as agent-callable tools), Constraint validation (physics + regulatory checks).

Domains: Construction planning, transportation systems, thermal engineering.

Key differentiator: Generalizable method — constraint-grounded agentic reasoning with simulation-in-the-loop — anchored by AlphaFold3/GNoME as precedents for AI-accelerated science.

SVF — Structured Validation Framework for Knowledge-Grounded Agent Systems

Core idea: Systematic evaluation infrastructure for knowledge-grounded and multi-agent AI systems, measuring properties that determine real-world reliability beyond point accuracy.

Four dimensions: Grounding integrity (retrieval relevance, attribution accuracy), Provenance traceability (span-level citation, audit trails), Reasoning robustness (noise, contradictions, adversarial inputs), Coordination reliability (agent consistency, failure recovery).

Systems evaluated: Vector RAG, hybrid retrieval, graph-augmented agents.

Key differentiator: First structured evaluation framework for agentic AI — embeds validation as a first-class component of agent execution pipelines, aligned with OpenTelemetry observability standards.

PA-AKG — Provenance-Aware Agentic Knowledge Graph Systems (Other Initiatives)

Core idea: Hybrid architecture integrating structured knowledge graphs, multi-source retrieval, and agent orchestration for reliable grounded reasoning with verifiable provenance.

Key contributions: Multi-source knowledge grounding, automated provenance generation, validation agents for retrieval failure recovery.

Key differentiator: Strongest fit for Knowledge Grounding venues — highly portable to NSF, DARPA, or other industry research programs.
