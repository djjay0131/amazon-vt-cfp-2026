The original submission strategy recommended SVF + AKG-E for Amazon, reserving PA-AKG for other initiatives. This page challenges that decision by advocating honestly for PA-AKG.

Verdict: PA-AKG Is Our Strongest Proposal

Our own data ranks PA-AKG #1 across every evaluation dimension. The arguments for excluding it were strategically reasonable but masked genuine weaknesses in SVF and AKG-E that this analysis surfaces.

Outcome: Strategy Revised to PA-AKG + AKG-E

Based on this analysis, the submission plan has been updated: PA-AKG (Knowledge Grounding) as the primary Amazon submission, AKG-E (AI for Science & Engineering) as the second. SVF is reserved for other evaluation-focused venues.

We ranked it #1 and then decided not to submit it. The strategic reasoning made sense on paper (topic overlap, portability), but the core fact remains: we chose not to submit our strongest-fit proposal.

2 "Already Productized" Actually Helps PA-AKG

The original strategy argued that Bedrock KB having hybrid search makes PA-AKG feel incremental. But flip it: Amazon knows retrieval deeply, which means their reviewers understand the remaining hard problems. They know Bedrock KB can't do:

  • Cross-source entity-level fusion (KG + DB + documents)
  • Fine-grained provenance (span-level, not document-level)
  • Validation agents that detect and recover from retrieval failures
  • Auditable evidence chains across multi-step agent workflows

PA-AKG targets exactly these gaps. It's easier to pitch an improvement to something Amazon uses daily than a brand-new engineering framework they don't have context for.

3 Feasibility: PA-AKG Is Most Deliverable in 1 Year

This is the strongest argument for PA-AKG and the most honest weakness of the other two.

AKG-E Requires in 1 Year:

  • Domain KGs for construction, transportation, AND thermal engineering
  • Simulator integrations (PDE solvers, FEA tools exposed as agent tools)
  • Constraint validation systems for each domain
  • Evaluation benchmarks across all three domains

That's extremely ambitious. Even getting ONE domain working well would be impressive. The abstract promises three.

SVF Requires in 1 Year:

  • A full evaluation toolkit
  • Benchmark tasks spanning scientific reasoning + enterprise decision-support
  • Agent failure scenario simulations
  • Structured provenance scoring metrics
  • "The first structured evaluation framework" — a very high bar

Good benchmarks are notoriously hard to build. GAIA and AgentBench took large teams.

PA-AKG Requires in 1 Year:

  • One hybrid retrieval+KG system with dynamic pathway selection
  • Provenance generation layer
  • Validation agents
  • Ablation benchmarks (vector RAG vs. hybrid vs. GraphRAG vs. PA-AKG)

This is a clearly scoped system with natural ablation points. Most deliverable of the three.

4 Honest Weaknesses of SVF

The "Meta" Problem

SVF evaluates systems but doesn't build one. Reviewers who want to see systems being built — not just measured — may find this less compelling. "We'll create an evaluation framework" doesn't have the same impact as "we'll build a system that does X."

Evaluation Dimensions Feel Obvious

"Grounding integrity, provenance traceability, reasoning robustness, coordination reliability" — a tough reviewer could say: "Obviously these matter. What's the research insight? What surprised you? What's the hypothesis being tested?" SVF describes what to measure but the why these four specifically is asserted, not derived.

"First Structured Evaluation Framework" Is an Overclaim

There are existing evaluation frameworks for RAG (RAGAS, ARES, TruLens). SVF's claim to novelty needs to be sharper — it's more like "first framework that evaluates grounding + provenance + coordination together for multi-agent systems." But that's a mouthful.

Coordination Reliability Is Underdeveloped

The fourth dimension is the weakest in the abstract. How do you actually benchmark "cascade prevention" or "failure recovery"? The approach is vague compared to the other three dimensions which have clearer metrics.

5 Honest Weaknesses of AKG-E

Our Own Report Warned About Reviewer Mismatch

The deep-research-topics.md says: "risk is reviewer mismatch if the proposal reads as 'general agent/KG' without a crisp scientific objective." AKG-E is a general framework. AI for Science reviewers expect domain breakthroughs like AlphaFold — systems that advance science, not tooling frameworks.

AlphaFold3/GNoME Anchoring Is a Stretch

Those are domain-specific scientific breakthroughs (protein folding, materials stability prediction). AKG-E is a general agent framework applied to engineering. The comparison actually highlights the gap: AlphaFold3 is the science; AKG-E is infrastructure for science. A reviewer could see this as apples to oranges.

"Constraint Violation Reduction Rates" Is Vague

Against what baseline? What specific engineering tasks? What constraints? The abstract names categories (BOM reasoning, schedule risk) but no concrete problem instances.

Simulation Integration Layer Is Hand-Waved

Exposing PDE solvers as "agent-callable tools" is a massive impedance mismatch problem. How does a natural language agent formulate inputs for a finite element solver? This is arguably a research project unto itself.

6 The Overlap Argument Is Overstated

The original strategy argued that PA-AKG + AKG-E sharing "Agentic AI" as secondary creates overlap risk. But honestly:

  • Different primary topics go to different reviewer pools. Knowledge Grounding reviewers are not the same as AI for Science reviewers.
  • "Agentic AI" as secondary is extremely common — it's the hottest category. Plenty of submissions will use it as secondary.
  • PA-AKG and AKG-E are genuinely different proposals — one about retrieval/provenance, one about simulation/engineering.

The overlap is cosmetic, not conceptual.

8 Honest Limitations of This Analysis

PA-AKG's Real Weakness: Crowded Space

"Better RAG with knowledge graphs" proposals are common. Even with the provenance angle, a reviewer who's seen 20 KG-RAG proposals might struggle to see PA-AKG as distinct. AKG-E, for all its risks, is distinctive — nobody else is proposing agentic engineering frameworks with simulation-in-the-loop.

The "Other Initiatives" Argument Was Valid

Knowledge Grounding IS broadly fundable. AKG-E and SVF are more niche and harder to place elsewhere. The Amazon CFP may genuinely be the best home for them.

AKG-E Has the Better Story

Even if AKG-E is riskier, "AI agents that can do engineering" is a more exciting narrative than "better provenance tracking for RAG." If one proposal gets invited to the full proposal stage, AKG-E has more room to grow into a compelling 3-4 page proposal.

Decision: Submit PA-AKG + AKG-E

PA-AKG is the safest, highest-ranked submission. AKG-E provides the distinctive, ambitious second slot with strong advisor alignment. SVF is reserved for evaluation-focused venues where its "meta" nature is a feature, not a weakness.
