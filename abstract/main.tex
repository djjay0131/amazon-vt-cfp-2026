\noindent\textbf{Primary Topic Area:} Knowledge Grounding \hfill
\textbf{Secondary Topic Area:} Agentic AI

\vspace{0.3em}

Large Language Models (LLMs) increasingly rely on retrieval mechanisms to incorporate external knowledge during inference. Most current retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) systems rely on vector similarity search over unstructured documents. While effective for relevance, these systems provide limited support for structured reasoning, provenance tracking, and verifiable evidence chains. As agentic AI systems become capable of autonomous planning and decision-making, this lack of grounded traceability introduces risks including hallucinated outputs, unverifiable reasoning, and brittle performance when operating across heterogeneous knowledge sources.

This project proposes \textbf{Provenance-Aware Agentic Knowledge Graph Systems (PA-AKG)}, a hybrid architecture that integrates knowledge graphs, multi-source retrieval, and agent orchestration to support reliable grounded reasoning. The core idea is to combine structured knowledge representations with generative models so that agent workflows can reason over entities, relationships, and constraints while maintaining explicit links to supporting evidence. In this framework, agents dynamically select between vector-based retrieval and structured knowledge graph queries, enabling hybrid reasoning workflows that balance semantic retrieval with symbolic structure.

The proposed system enables \textbf{multi-source knowledge grounding across heterogeneous environments}, including knowledge graphs, relational databases, scientific literature, and domain-specific document collections. Agents generate reasoning chains that explicitly reference structured entities and supporting evidence. A key component of the system is an automated \textbf{provenance generation and validation layer}, which produces traceable reasoning paths linking model outputs to underlying knowledge sources. This capability enables both automated and human verification of agent outputs, improving reliability in tasks that require multi-step reasoning.

This research will investigate several core questions:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item How can heterogeneous knowledge sources---including knowledge graphs, databases, and document corpora---be integrated into a unified grounding framework for generative systems?
    \item How should agent workflows dynamically select between structured and unstructured retrieval pathways during complex reasoning tasks?
    \item What mechanisms enable scalable provenance generation and verification for agent-generated outputs?
    \item How does structured knowledge grounding affect reasoning accuracy, robustness, and computational efficiency compared to conventional RAG systems?
\end{enumerate}

To answer these questions, we will design a \textbf{hybrid KG--RAG architecture}, implement agent orchestration workflows that combine structured reasoning with generative models, and develop evaluation benchmarks that measure grounded reasoning quality, provenance completeness, and system efficiency. Experimental evaluation will compare three paradigms: (1)~vector-based RAG systems, (2)~hybrid structured retrieval pipelines, and (3)~fully knowledge-graph-driven agentic reasoning architectures.

The research will be evaluated in representative scenarios requiring reliable knowledge grounding and complex reasoning, including \textbf{scientific research discovery and engineering decision-support tasks}. These domains require integrating heterogeneous knowledge sources, structured constraints, and multi-step reasoning processes.

Expected outcomes include:
\begin{itemize}
    \item A novel architecture for \textbf{provenance-aware knowledge-grounded agentic AI systems}
    \item Methods for integrating structured knowledge graphs with generative retrieval pipelines
    \item Evaluation benchmarks for grounded reasoning and provenance validation in agentic systems
    \item An open research prototype enabling reproducible experimentation and future extensions
\end{itemize}

By enabling generative AI systems to reason over structured knowledge while maintaining explicit evidence chains, this work aims to significantly improve the \textbf{reliability, transparency, and robustness of agentic AI systems operating over complex real-world knowledge environments}.
